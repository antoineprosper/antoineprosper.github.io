Research Interests

My research focuses on visual plasticity using psychophysics and neuroimaging to quantify and compare the plasticity of the visual system in and across humans. I have a particular interest in combining imaging data with computational neuroscience approaches and machine learning techniques to advance our understanding of neural mechanisms in humans.

Ideal project

I am interested in inducing sensory stimulation in the brain using non-invasive stimulation techniques. I would like to model the process of how sensory input travels through the sensory cortices and gets integrated into a coherent percept in later areas, and how we can use this representation to induce "artificial" sensory experiences in a brain-computer interface.

Open Science & Scientific Conviction

I believe science works best when it is open, collaborative, and reproducible. Sharing data, methods, and results not only accelerates discovery but also helps everyone build on each other's work. I am committed to making my research as accessible and transparent as possible, and strive to design reproducible and transparent experiments and analyses. Negative results are crucial to build a coherent body of knowledge, and the current bias towards positive results is highly detrimental to the scientific process.
